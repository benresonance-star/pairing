Design Upgrade Levers — Revenue vs Build Cost Net Gain
Each design upgrade shows its individual build cost vs sale value uplift — so you can see exactly which upgrades earn their keep and which don't. Net gain = (sale uplift × 3 units sold) − (build cost × 4 units).
2.7m ceilings (raised from 2.4m)
OFF
+$0 net gain
Build cost: +$8k/TH (+$32k total)
Sale uplift: +$40k/TH
300mm of extra ceiling height is the single highest ROI design decision. Buyers perceive it as luxury; it costs ~$8K in extra wall framing and plasterboard. Net margin gain per sold unit: ~$32K.
Engineered timber floors + stone benchtops
OFF
+$0 net gain
Build cost: +$15k/TH (+$60k total)
Sale uplift: +$55k/TH
The two finishes buyers inspect most carefully. Engineered timber over carpet adds ~$8K; 40mm stone benchtops over laminate adds ~$7K. Combined they lift comparable sale price by $50–60K in this market. Net margin gain per sold unit: ~$40K.
Double garage (instead of single)
OFF
+$0 net gain
Build cost: +$25k/TH (+$100k total)
Sale uplift: +$70k/TH
Hampton East families consistently cite a double garage as a non-negotiable. Widens your buyer pool from couples to families. The footprint increase (~20m²) adds build cost but the sale premium is disproportionately higher. Net margin gain per sold unit: ~$45K.
3-bed + study (market as 4-bed)
OFF

A dedicated study or 4th bedroom on a mezzanine or ground floor opens an entirely different buyer pool. Families paying $1.35M+ almost always require 4 bedrooms. Net margin gain per sold unit: ~$57K.

CGT & Tax Considerations
Key structuring points — verify with your accountant
Company structure (recommended): Development profit taxed at 30% (or 25% base rate entity) vs up to 47% personal. Significant difference on a $400–600K profit.

Retained TH — CGT discount: If the retained townhouse is held for >12 months before any future sale, and the ATO treats it on capital account (not revenue), the 50% CGT discount may apply. Revenue account treatment is more likely given development intent — get advice early.

GST on retained unit: A property held for residential rental is input-taxed — no GST charged on rent, and no GST on a future private sale (if sold as going concern or after long-term hold). This removes ~$40–60K of GST obligation vs selling it.

Refinance on completion: Once townhouses complete, refinance the retained unit to a standard residential investment loan at ~6–6.5% — far cheaper than the construction facility rate. This significantly improves the holding cost.

Understanding this chart During construction your gross asset value grows as work-in-progress accumulates, but your debt also peaks. Net equity may dip before recovering sharply as sales settle. This is normal in development — the key is ensuring your peak debt never exceeds the lender's LVR ceiling, and that your first settlement covers your most expensive debt (mezzanine first, then vendor finance).
